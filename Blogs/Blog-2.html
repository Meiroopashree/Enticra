Newborn Surgeries: Ensuring a Healthy Start for Your Baby

We specialize in newborn surgeries to address congenital conditions that may require immediate medical attention. Our skilled surgeons employ advanced techniques and state-of-the-art equipment to ensure your baby receives the best possible care.

What are Newborn Surgeries?

Newborn surgeries, also known as neonatal surgeries, are performed shortly after birth to correct congenital malformations or medical conditions that could impact your child's health. Common conditions requiring surgery include:

  • Congenital Heart Defects: Such as holes in the heart or abnormal valves.
  • Cleft Lip and Palate: Reconstructive surgery to restore function and appearance.
  • Intestinal Obstructions: Blockages preventing proper digestion, like Hirschsprung's disease.
  • Diaphragmatic Hernia: Repairing a hole in the diaphragm to support lung development.
  • Esophageal Atresia: Connecting the esophagus for normal feeding.

What to Expect During Surgery

Our team performs highly specialized procedures, beginning with a thorough diagnosis often made prenatally. During surgery, your baby is closely monitored by paediatric anaesthesiologists to ensure safety and comfort. We prioritize clear communication with parents, providing support from diagnosis through recovery.

Recovery and Post-Surgical Care

Post-surgery, newborns are monitored in a neonatal intensive care unit (NICU). The length of the hospital stay varies based on the surgery's complexity and the baby’s health. Our team provides comprehensive post-operative care, including feeding guidance and recognizing signs of complications.
