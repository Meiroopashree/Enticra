Understanding Paediatric Urology

Paediatric urology addresses a variety of conditions, including:

  • Urinary Tract Infections (UTIs): Frequent UTIs may indicate underlying issues in the urinary system.
  • Hypospadias: A congenital defect where the urethra opens on the underside of the penis, requiring surgical correction.
  • Vesicoureteral Reflux (VUR): Urine flows backward from the bladder to the kidneys, potentially leading to UTIs and kidney damage.
  • Undescended Testes (Cryptorchidism): Surgical intervention may be necessary if one or both testicles have not descended into the scrotum.
  • Bladder Control Problems: Conditions like bed-wetting can often be managed with behavioral therapies or medication.
  • Kidney Stones: Though less common in children, they can cause significant pain and may require treatment.

Treatment Options

Treatment plans are tailored to each child's unique needs and may include:

  • Medication and Behavioral Therapies: For mild conditions like bed-wetting.
  • Minimally Invasive Surgery: For conditions like hypospadias and undescended testes, promoting quicker recovery.
  • Endoscopic Procedures: Addressing urinary tract abnormalities without large incisions.

Post-Treatment Care

After treatment, we provide thorough post-operative care and regular follow-up visits to monitor recovery and manage any concerns. We also offer guidance for parents on home care, diet, and exercises to support bladder control.
